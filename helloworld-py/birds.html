Northern Cardinal

(Cardinalis cardinalis) is a medium-sized songbird with a distinctive crest on its head, and is found throughout South Carolina. Males are bright red with black around their faces while females are a soft brown with a reddish tinge.

Carolina Wren

(Thryothorus ludovicianus) is a small, round-bodied bird with a distinctive long, curved bill and a perky tail that is often held upright. It is known for its loud, musical song and can be found in woodlands, backyards, and parks throughout South Carolina.

American Goldfinch

(Spinus tristis) is a small, yellow-and-black bird that is often seen in flocks feeding on seeds in fields and gardens. During the winter, its plumage changes to a drab olive-brown color, but in the spring, the male's feathers become a brilliant yellow.

Mourning Dove

(Zenaida macroura) is a medium-sized, plump bird with a long, pointed tail that is found throughout South Carolina. It is known for its soft, mournful cooing and is often seen perched on telephone wires and in trees.

Tufted Titmouse

(Baeolophus bicolor) is a small, gray bird with a distinctive crest on its head and a black patch above its bill. It is a common backyard bird in South Carolina and is known for its curious and acrobatic behavior.

Blue Jay

(Cyanocitta cristata) is a striking, blue-and-white bird with a prominent crest on its head. It is known for its loud, harsh calls and is often seen raiding bird feeders and nestling boxes in backyards throughout South Carolina.

Eastern Bluebird

(Sialia sialis) is a small, colorful bird with a blue back, reddish breast, and white belly. It is often seen perched on wires and fences or flying low over fields and meadows in South Carolina.

Red-bellied Woodpecker

(Melanerpes carolinus) is a medium-sized woodpecker with a red cap and a black-and-white striped back. It is often heard before it is seen, as it has a distinctive rolling call, and is found in woodlands, parks, and suburban areas throughout South Carolina.

White-breasted Nuthatch

(Sitta carolinensis) is a small, stocky bird with a distinctive black cap and a white face and belly. It is often seen moving headfirst down tree trunks and branches in search of insects and seeds in South Carolina.

Brown-headed Nuthatch

(Sitta pusilla) is a small, short-tailed bird with a distinctive blue-gray back and a rusty-brown head. It is found in pine forests throughout South Carolina and is known for its comical, nasal calls and acrobatic climbing abilities.

(Thryothorus ludovicianus) is a small, round-bodied bird with a distinctive long, curved bill and a perky tail that is often held upright. It is known for its loud, musical song and can be found in woodlands, backyards, and parks throughout South Carolina.
